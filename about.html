About

I was born in Ohio (USA) and moved to Belgium at a young age where I studied at de! Kunsthumaniora Hedendaagse Dans (BE), focusing on contemporary dance. Afterwards, in ArtEZ Hogeschool voor de Kunsten (NL), I developed my skills as a choreographer and researcher and created several pieces and performed in various festivals in the Netherlands.

Upon graduating, I worked for Cristina Planas Leitão and Cinzia Scoglionero, amongst others, and worked within Strange Strangers, as the co-founder, choreographer, and dancer of the organisation. Together with Danielle Huyghe we created Out of the blue. This piece is touring internationally, has received the jury prize for best choreography at Festival 25 Masdanza, has been selected for international competitions and has been recognised by Aerowaves within the Springback Magazine as 40 winks.

Individually, I have been conducting a performance research named (Get a room) since 2020 which questions the paradox of being private in public within a performance. Two projects have since emerged from this research, being trial out or err_or and (Peek show). The research project until now has been supported by Noud Heerkens, Diane Elshout, ArtEZ, …ism project space and HOOGTIJ.

Throughout the years of being immersed in the performing arts I noticed a need to explore the body in different ways than I had been educated. This curiosity led me to wanting to understand more about the notion of awareness and how we think. Since recently, I have been self-studying neuroscience, psychology, and human behavior which has led me to teaching movement classes at Tree of Life Brussels (BE) and Yoga Coolhaven (NL).

Currently, I am working as a freelance artist and teacher between The Netherlands and Belgium.
